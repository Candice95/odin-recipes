Takoyaki

Crispy homemade takoyaki

Takoyaki (たこ焼き) is a ball shaped snack made popular in Japan. Originating and popularized in Osaka this dish consists of a wheat flour based batter often filled with chopped octopus, green onions tempura scraps, pickled ginger and topped with mayonnaise, takoyaki sauce, bonito flakes, and dried seaweed.

Ingredients

Cooking takoyaki and batter

  • 1/4 cup of dried bonito flakes
  • 2 green onions or scallions
  • 1 tbsp pickled red ginger
  • 4.2oz of octopus sashimi
  • 1/3 cup of tempura scraps
  • 1 cup all purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 cup of dashi (japanese soup stock)

Garnishing Takoyaki

  • 1/2 cup of takoyaki sauce
  • japanese mayonnaise
  • dried bonito flakes
  • dried green seaweed

Cooking Instructions

  1. Grind ¼ cup katsuobushi (bonito flakes) into a fine powder. Set aside, we'll use this powder when we're cooking Takoyaki.
  2. Cut green onions into fine slices and mince 1 Tbsp red pickled ginger.
  3. Cut the octopus into ½ inch (1.3 cm) bite-size pieces
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine 1 cup (120 g) all-purpose flour, 2 tsp baking powder, and ½ tsp kosher salt and whisk all together.
  5. Add 2 large eggs, 1 tsp soy sauce, and 1 ½ cup (360 ml) dashi.
  6. Whisk all together until well-blended and transfer the batter to a 2-cup measuring cup
  7. Heat the takoyaki pan to 400ºF (200ºC) over medium heat. Using a brush, generously oil the takoyaki pan
  8. Add 1-3 octopus pieces in each hole depending on its size and sprinkle katsuobushi powder you ground earlier on top.
  9. Then sprinkle tenkasu, green onion, and pickled red ginger.
  10. After 3 minutes, flip and rotate the battered balls until they are fully cooked on all sides
  11. Transfer the takoyaki onto a plate and add toppings to your liking!
